Factual Turtle Twitterbot

The NAU ACM club took a large group to LA Hacks this Spring. My team wanted to practice using Python and make use of an API none of us had used before. We accomplished this by making the Factual Turtle Twitterbot, a Python webapp that generates tweets about turtles.

The sentences are made from madlib skeletons and wordbanks that match to parts of speech. When a sentence has been made, it is processed by a mashing algortihm that misspells words like a human would with accidental keystrokes. Then it is tweeted to the account named by the user.

Not including the uniqueness of spelling mistakes, the app is capable of generating more than 1.4 billion sentences.

@TurtleDynasty It's Turtlle Tuesday. Don't forget to walk intelligently!

— Factual Turtle (@factualturtle) April 17, 2014

Turtle Randomizer

During spare time of my sophomore year, I wrote a javascript program that randomly colors a turtle's shell. It works by searching for ids inside components of a scalable vector graphic (SVG) of the turtle. Check it out here.
